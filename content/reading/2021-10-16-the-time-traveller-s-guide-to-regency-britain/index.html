Review

In the latest instalment of his Time Traveller’s Guide series of social history books, Ian Mortimer guides us through day-to-day life in Britain during the wider Regency period of 1779-1830. We are transported back in time to the world of Wellington & Napoleon, Beethoven and Mozart, Wordsworth and Shelley to learn what their lives, the lives of Kings and Queens and the lives of working folk were like, how they functioned and why things were as they were.

Where would you stay and how much would it cost if you arrived in London in March 1803? What would you eat and drink? How would you go about posting a letter or travelling between towns? What would you do, where would you go and what could be done if you were taken ill? These are the kind of questions this book answers. The scope and detail is encyclopaedic but, under the clever guise of a guide book, the narrative never becomes a boring list of facts - a trap which all too many history books fall into. Instead we have a book filled with character and personality and a very entertaining and easy read.

Rating ⭑⭑⭑
